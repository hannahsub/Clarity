Clarity – Privacy Policy

Effective: 15 September 2025

Clarity tracks and blocks AI sites to help you focus. It is designed to work entirely on your device. It does not run servers, and your data will not be sold, rented, or shared.

Summary
  • No collection: No collection or transmission of personal data to any server.
  • Local-only: Usage stats and settings are stored in your browser via chrome.storage.local and chrome.storage.sync.
  • Your control: You can remove the extension any time.

Data the extension stores locally

  • AI usage minutes per day (aggregated seconds per domain and date).
  • Custom domains you add to block/track (synced if you’re signed into Chrome).
  • Focus/class timers (start & end times) and installed date (for streaks).

Clarity records only hostnames (e.g., chatgpt.com) and time totals. It does not read page content, keystrokes, chat messages, or cookies.

How data is processed

Clarity’s content script notes when a tab on a tracked domain is visible and credits time locally. Blocking uses Chrome’s declarativeNetRequest and does not send URLs off-device.

Sharing & third parties

Your data will not be shared with third parties. The extension includes no analytics, ads, or trackers.

Permissions explained

  • declarativeNetRequest: block configured sites during a focus session.
  • storage: save your settings and local usage totals.
  • tabs/windows/idle/alarms/scripting: implement blocking, timers, and local time tracking.
  • host permissions (<all_urls>): required to apply blocking rules and detect hostnames. Does not read page content.

Retention & deletion

Data remains in your browser until you remove the extension or clear its storage. To delete:

  • Remove the extension (deletes local data), or

Incognito

If you enable Allow in incognito, Chrome may keep separate storage for incognito. Removing the extension deletes that data too.

Children

Clarity is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly process children’s personal data.
